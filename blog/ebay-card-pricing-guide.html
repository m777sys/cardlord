Pokemon vs One Piece vs Sports Cards

Different card types, different pricing dynamics:

Pokemon Cards

  • High liquidity: Easy to price, market is efficient
  • Volatility: Prices swing hard (new sets, banlists, reprints)
  • PSA premium: Graded cards command 2-10x raw prices
  • Key metric: PriceCharting + PSA pop reports

One Piece Cards

  • Growing market: Less price efficiency, more opportunity
  • Regional variance: Japanese vs English vs Italian pricing differs
  • Meta-dependent: Tournament results impact prices fast
  • Key metric: Recent eBay sales + TCGPlayer data

Sports Cards (Baseball/Football/Basketball)

  • Seasonal: Playoff runs spike prices
  • Condition sensitive: Centering/off-center kills value
  • Vintage vs modern: Completely different markets
  • Key metric: 130point, CardLadder for high-end

When to Lower Prices (And When to Wait)

Here's my decision framework:

Lower immediately if:
  • Your price > 30% above market (using PriceCharting or sold data)
  • 0 views in 48 hours on a fresh listing
  • Same card (same grade) selling below your price
Hold if:
  • Your price is within 10% of market
  • Card is rare/population < 100
  • Market is trending up (check last 30 days)

Pro tip: Set a calendar reminder to check prices every 2 weeks on stagnant listings. Don't "set and forget."

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I update prices?

For cards under $50: Every 2 weeks. For $50-$500: Weekly. For $500+: Daily during volatile periods. Market moves fast on chase cards.

Is PriceCharting accurate?

For high-volume cards: Yes, it's excellent. For low-population (<50 sales/month): Use it as a starting point, then check actual recent eBay sales. PriceCharting can lag 24-48 hours on fast movers.

Should I use fixed price or auction?

Fixed price 95% of the time. Reserve auctions for:
‚Ä¢ Graded cards >$500
‚Ä¢ Authenticated/vintage cards
‚Ä¢ When you genuinely don't know market value

How do I handle "Best Offer"?

Turn it off for cards under $30 (not worth the negotiation time). For $30-100: Auto-decline below 80% of asking. For $100+: Consider offers down to 85% if the card has been listed 14+ days.

What's the best time to list cards?

Sunday evenings (7-10 PM EST) consistently show highest sell-through. Avoid Friday/Saturday afternoons when people are out. For sports cards: list during the season, not offseason.

1. The 30-Day Spike Pattern
Card jumped 30%+ in 30 days with no fundamental change (no new set, no tournament win). This is hype. Hype fades. Take profits.
2. Tournament Banlist Preview
If a card dominates tournaments, it's on the banlist radar. Sell before the announcement tanks value.
3. You're Up 100%+
Doubled your money? Take half off the table. Keep half as "house money." Sleep better, lock gains.
4. Re-Print Confirmed
Announcement of reprint = immediate price drop. Sell the rumor, not the news.
5. Cash Flow Emergency
Real opportunity costs. If you need capital for a better flip or business expense, liquidate.

1. Low Population Scarcity

2. Cultural Moment Building

3. Meta Dominance
Card is core to Tier 1 competitive deck. As long as it's playable, demand stays.
4. You Bought the Dip
You purchased after a 30%+ price drop on good news (reprint, temporary dip). You timed the cycle.

Sell vs Hold by Card Type

Pokemon Cards

SELL Modern sets (2020+): Print runs are massive. Prices trend down over time. Take profits on spikes.

HOLD WOTC era (1999-2003): True scarcity. Population is fixed. Long-term appreciation.

One Piece Cards

HOLD OP01-OP03: Small print runs. Early sets in a growing game. Similar to Base Set Pokemon in 1999.

SELL New OP05+ singles: Supply is high. Prices volatile. Flip fast or watch them drop.

Sports Cards

SELL Modern rookies: Overproduction era. Most won't hold value. Cash out on hot starts.

HOLD Vintage (pre-1980): Genuine scarcity. Iconic players. Institutional buyers entering.

Understanding Card Market Cycles

Card prices follow predictable patterns. Learn the cycle:

The Card Market Cycle:

1. Hype Phase: New release, viral moment, price spike
2. Correction Phase:> Early sellers take profits, price drops 20-40%
3. Stabilization: True demand emerges, price finds floor
4. Growth Phase: Gradual appreciation (or) slow decline

Strategy: Buy in Correction, Sell in Hype

Example: Charizard VMAX (Shining Fates)

  • Hype Peak (Feb 2021): $400
  • Correction (Apr 2021): $180 (-55%)
  • Stabilized (Sep 2021): $120-150
  • Current: ~$80-100

If you held from peak, you're down 75%. If you sold at $350, you captured the move.
